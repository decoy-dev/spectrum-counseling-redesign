Payment & Insurance

Dr. Haddox is considered out-of-network for all insurance plans and does not submit claims directly to insurance companies. However, if you would like to submit a claim to your insurance company for reimbursement, we can assist by providing a diagnosis and procedure code.

Payment is accepted via cash, check, or credit card. Payment is expected at the time services are rendered.

Cancellation Policy

If you are unable to keep an appointment, please notify the therapist immediately. Appointments cancelled without 24-hour notice will be billed at a rate of $75.

Third and subsequent late cancellations, as well as appointments missed without any notice, will be billed the full session fee. These fees may be charged to the credit card on file.

Good Faith Estimate

For clients who choose to self-pay without applying for out-of-network reimbursement, you have the right to receive a "Good Faith Estimate" explaining how much your medical care will cost. Under the law, health care providers need to give clients who don't have insurance or who are not using insurance an estimate of the bill for medical items and services.

  • description You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of any non-emergency items or services, including related costs like medical tests, prescription drugs, equipment, and hospital fees.
  • calendar_today Make sure your health care provider gives you a Good Faith Estimate in writing at least 1 business day before your medical service or item. You can also ask any provider for a Good Faith Estimate before you schedule an item or service.
  • gavel If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you can dispute the bill.

For questions or more information about your right to a Good Faith Estimate, visit www.cms.gov/nosurprises.

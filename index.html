About Dr. Marie Haddox, Ph.D.

With over 20 years of clinical experience, Dr. Marie Haddox, Ph.D. has dedicated her practice to creating a holistic sanctuary where clients feel seen, heard, and deeply understood. Her commitment is rooted in the belief that a safe, therapeutic relationship is the foundation of all meaningful healing.

Dr. Haddox brings a wealth of expertise from her tenure at Banner Desert Medical Center and her advanced clinical training. She earned her Bachelor of Science from Arizona State University, followed by her Master's and Ph.D. from USIU San Diego.

At Spectrum Counseling, we navigate the complexities of modern identity, relationship dynamics, and family structures with an evidence-based yet intuitive approach.

LGBTQ+ Affirming Therapy

We provide a deeply affirming space for the LGBTQ+ community. Dr. Haddox specializes in gender identity, transitioning support, and the unique challenges faced by queer individuals and couples. You don't have to explain your identity here—you are celebrated for it.

Therapy Specializations

Deep clinical expertise in depression, anxiety, relationships, sexuality, and gender identity counseling in Gilbert, AZ.

We work with individuals experiencing persistent sadness, loss of motivation, and emotional numbness. Dr. Haddox uses cognitive-behavioral and psychodynamic approaches to uncover root causes and develop sustainable coping strategies tailored to each person.

From generalized anxiety to panic disorders and social anxiety, we provide evidence-based treatment integrating mindfulness techniques with traditional therapeutic methods to help you regain control and build resilience.

We help couples and individuals navigate communication breakdowns, trust issues, codependency, and attachment patterns. Recovery from betrayal, improving family dynamics, and building healthier connections are areas of deep expertise.

We offer a judgment-free space to discuss sexual orientation, intimacy concerns, and the intersection of identity and desire. Support includes questions of identity, coming out, and building fulfilling intimate connections.

We support clients questioning, exploring, or affirming their gender — including those considering or undergoing transition. Our practice provides letters of support and guidance through social and medical transitions.

Insurance & Billing

Insurance Coverage at Spectrum Counseling

We are currently in-network with Blue Cross Blue Shield and Aetna. For other providers, we can provide a "superbill" for potential out-of-network reimbursement. We're happy to help you navigate your benefits before your first appointment.

Getting Started

Your First Session with Dr. Haddox

The initial consultation is an intake session where we discuss your history, goals, and determine if our practice is the right fit for your needs. This session typically lasts 60 minutes, and there's no pressure — it's a conversation to help us both understand how we can work together most effectively.

Session Details

How Long Are Counseling Sessions?

Standard individual sessions are 50 minutes. Couples and family sessions are typically 60–75 minutes depending on the complexity of the dynamic. Extended sessions can be arranged for intensive therapeutic work when needed.

Virtual Care

Telehealth Therapy Across Arizona

Yes, we offer secure telehealth sessions for clients throughout Arizona. Virtual sessions follow the same structure as in-person appointments and are conducted through a HIPAA-compliant platform. Many clients find the flexibility of telehealth helpful for maintaining consistency in their care.

Policies

Our Cancellation Policy

We ask for at least 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule your appointment. Late cancellations or no-shows may be subject to a fee. We understand that life happens and will always work with you when unexpected circumstances arise.
